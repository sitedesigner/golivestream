## Follow-Up Email Templates

### Prospect Stage — Initial Outreach

**Subject: Quick question about [Company]'s [specific goal/challenge]**

```
Hi [First Name],

I noticed [specific observation about their company — recent funding, new product launch, 
hiring spree, etc.]. Congrats on the momentum!

At [Your Company], we help companies like [similar company/reference] achieve 
[specific outcome] — typically [specific metric/result].

Would you be open to a 15-minute call this week to see if there's a fit? 
I'm flexible Tuesday or Thursday afternoon.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Call Scripts

### Qualified Stage — Discovery/Qualification Call

**Opening (2 minutes)**
> "Hi [Name], thanks for making time today. I know you're busy, so I want to make this 
> valuable. My goal today is to understand your [specific challenge] better and see if 
> what we've built could help. Sound good?"

**Discovery Questions (10-15 minutes)**
1. "Walk me through how you currently handle [process/challenge]."
2. "What's working well? What's the biggest pain point?"
3. "How is this impacting your team/business? Any quantifiable impact?"
4. "Have you tried solving this before? What happened?"
5. "What would success look like for you in 6 months?"
6. "Who else is involved in evaluating solutions like this?"
7. "What's your timeline for making a decision?"
8. "Is there a budget allocated for this?"

**Qualifying (BANT)**
- **Budget:** "Do you have budget set aside for this?"
- **Authority:** "Who would ultimately sign off on this?"
- **Need:** "How critical is solving this for your team?"
- **Timeline:** "When do you need this resolved?"

**Next Steps (3 minutes)**
> "Based on what you've shared, I think there's a strong fit. Here's what I'd propose: 
> [specific next step — demo, proposal, technical review]. Can we schedule that for 
> [suggest date]?"

**Close the Call**
> "I'll send over a summary of what we discussed and [next deliverable] by [date]. 
> What's the best way to reach you if questions come up before then?"

---

### Proposal Stage — Proposal Review Call

**Opening (2 minutes)**
> "Hi [Name], I'm calling to walk through the proposal and make sure everything aligns 
> with what we discussed. I've blocked 30 minutes — is that enough time?"

**Review (10-15 minutes)**
1. "Let me start with the executive summary — does this match your expectations?"
2. "Here's what I'd highlight as the key differentiators for your situation..."
3. "Let's look at the implementation timeline — does this work for your team?"
4. "On the pricing — I want to make sure the ROI is clear. Here's how we calculate it..."

**Address Concerns (5 minutes)**
> "Before we wrap up, what questions or concerns do you have?"
> "Is there anything you'd like us to adjust?"

**Decision Process (3 minutes)**
> "What's the internal process for moving forward from here?"
> "Is there anyone else who should be part of this conversation?"
> "What would you need to see to feel confident moving forward?"

**Next Steps (2 minutes)**
> "Great — I'll [specific action] by [date]. Let's reconnect on [date] to discuss. 
> Does that work?"

## Objection Handling Reference

### Common Objections & Responses

| Objection | Response Strategy | Key Phrase |
|-----------|------------------|------------|
| "Too expensive" | Reframe to ROI, compare to cost of inaction | "I understand — let's look at what NOT solving this costs you..." |
| "We need to think about it" | Identify the real concern, create urgency | "Totally fair. What specific aspect do you want to think through?" |
| "We're happy with our current solution" | Find gaps, show differentiation | "That's great. One thing our clients often found with [current solution] was..." |
| "The timing isn't right" | Quantify the cost of delay, plant a seed | "I get it. What would need to change for the timing to be right?" |
| "I need to talk to my boss/team" | Offer to help with internal selling | "Would it help if I put together a business case you can share with them?" |
| "Your product doesn't have [feature]" | Acknowledge, pivot to value, roadmap | "That's a fair point. Here's how our clients have worked around that..." |
| "We had a bad experience with similar" | Empathize, differentiate | "I'm sorry to hear that. Here's what's different about our approach..." |
| "Send me more information" | Qualify the interest, don't just email | "Happy to — what specifically would be most useful for you?" |
| "We're going with a competitor" | Respect, understand, stay in touch | "They're a great company. May I ask what tipped the scales in their favor?" |
| "I'm too busy right now" | Respect time, offer value, reschedule | "I completely respect your time. Would a 10-minute call next week work?" |

### The LAIR Framework for Objection Handling

1. **Listen** — Let them finish completely. Don't interrupt.
2. **Acknowledge** — "That makes total sense. I can see why that would be a concern."
3. **Investigate** — "Can you help me understand more about [specific concern]?"
4. **Respond** — Address with specific evidence, case studies, or adjustments.

## Close Scripts

### Negotiation Stage — Closing Techniques

#### The Assumptive Close
> "Great, it sounds like we're aligned on the key terms. Let me get the contract over to 
> you by [date]. Should I send it to you directly, or does [other person] need to be 
> included?"

#### The Urgency Close
> "I want to be transparent — we only take on [X] new clients per quarter to ensure 
> quality onboarding. We have one slot left this quarter. If we can get the paperwork 
> started this week, I can hold that for you."

#### The Summary Close
> "Let me recap what we've agreed on:
> - [Term 1: e.g., 12-month agreement]
> - [Term 2: e.g., $X/month]
> - [Term 3: e.g., Implementation starts [date]]
> - [Term 4: e.g., Dedicated onboarding manager]
> 
> Does that capture everything? If so, I can have the agreement ready by [date]."

#### The "What Would It Take" Close
> "I sense we're close. Is there anything else you'd need to feel confident moving forward 
> today?"

#### The Takeaway Close
> "It sounds like this might not be the right fit right now. I'd rather you feel 100% 
> confident than rush into something. Let's revisit this [timeframe] when the timing 
> might be better."

#### The Ben Franklin Close
> "Let's list the pros and cons together. On the pro side: [list their stated benefits]. 
> On the con side: [acknowledge their concerns]. When you look at it this way, which 
> side feels heavier?"

## Quick Reference: When to Use What

| Stage | Primary Channel | Frequency | Goal |
|-------|----------------|-----------|------|
| Prospect | Email/LinkedIn | Every 3-5 days | Get first meeting |
| Contacted | Email + Call | Every 3-4 days | Complete demo/qualify |
| Qualified | Call + Email | Every 5-7 days | Send proposal |
| Proposal | Call + Email | Every 3-5 days | Address concerns, get commitment |
| Negotiation | Call (primary) | Every 1-2 days | Close deal |
| Closed-Won | Email + Call | Per onboarding plan | Ensure successful start |
| Closed-Lost | Email | Quarterly | Re-engage when timing changes |
