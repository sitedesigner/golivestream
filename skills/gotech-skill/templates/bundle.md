## Pain Points

1. **Too many vendors** — Managing 5+ separate agencies/freelancers for different growth functions; coordination overhead
2. **No full-stack growth** — Marketing, ops, content, CRO all siloed; no unified strategy or execution
3. **Fragmented data** — Each vendor uses different tools; no single source of truth for growth metrics
4. **High coordination cost** — Leadership spending 10+ hrs/week managing vendors instead of building product
5. **Scaling complexity** — What worked at $1M doesn't work at $5M; need integrated growth infrastructure

Instead of juggling five different agencies, freelancers, and tools for ops, content, comms, CRO, and analytics — get it all under one roof with unified strategy, shared data, and coordinated execution. One invoice, one point of contact, one integrated growth engine.

## Objection Handling

### 1. "Too expensive / We don't have budget"

"I get the sticker shock — $3,500/mo is real money. But look at what you're currently spending across fragmented vendors: most founders we work with are spending $8K–$25K/month when they add it all up. The Bundle usually saves 40–60% on total vendor spend while delivering better integrated results. Can I show you the math?"

### 2. "Now is not the right time"

"Fair enough. Most founders hit a breaking point when they're spending more time managing vendors than building product. What would need to change for this to become a priority?"

### 3. "We have preferred vendors we trust"

"Great — trust is everything. We're not asking you to fire vendors you love. But ask yourself: is there one area that's underperforming or creating coordination overhead? We can start there — swap in one service, prove the model, and expand from there."

### 4. "Bundles mean less flexibility"

"Actually, the opposite. With one integrated team, we can pivot faster than coordinating 5 separate vendors. If your priorities change next quarter, we adjust in days, not weeks of vendor meetings. The Bundle is actually MORE flexible than the alternative."

### 5. "I need to talk to my partners/team"

"Absolutely. Want me to create a side-by-side comparison showing your current vendor stack vs. the Bundle — cost, coordination time, and expected output? Makes the internal conversation much easier when they can see the specifics."
